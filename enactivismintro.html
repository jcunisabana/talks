In the 20th century, the idea that the mind is an immaterial substance mysteriously connected to the body was firmly rejected.

Cases where the senses deceive us are part of the motivation that Descartes presents to highlight the difference between the mind and the body, more precisely, the mind and matter.
According to the traditional model, the body and the environment are important for cognition but not its center.

Let's see this with a metaphor presented by Susan Hurley (1998).

Perception and action are the two paradigmatic forms of interaction with the body and the environment. And in the traditional model, these do not take a central place. Perhaps they are necessary for cognition but do not CONSTITUTE it; that's not where the meat is.
According to the 4E model, the body and the environment are important for cognition AND its center.
What does it mean that the body and the environment are the center of cognition?
